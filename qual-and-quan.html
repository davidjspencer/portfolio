Qualitative and Quantitative Research Informing UI

Using Analytics to Inform UX Design

AnalyticsUsability TestingUI

Challenge

A case study of the Google Merchandise Store using qualitative and quantitative research to improve the e-Commerce. The timeframe of this project was 2 weeks.


This page is currently under construction.

Findings

After distilling data from Google Analytics and creating usability tests to confirm these observations, I discovered that surprisingly the Google Merchandise Store has a lot of room for improvement. The focus of this case study is on the user flow of finding products and the check-out process, other areas of the site require attention but are outside the scope of this project.

Software used

  • Google Analytics
  • Illustrator
  • InDesign
  • QuickTime Player (to record usability tests)

Who are our users and how do they shop?

Before creating the usability tests, I wanted to gather data to see who our users are and how do they interact with the site. Perhaps not surprising to find is that over a third of sessions are from desktop devices.

Considering the ratio of desktop sessions to mobile it was a little surprising to find that the majority of transactions actually come from mobile.

I delved into this phenomenon a little further, so I looked at the sessions at each time of day and overlayed it on to the transactions.

Because I wasn't accessing the Google Analytics as admin (Google gives everyone access to their merchandise store's analytics) I couldn't track people across devices, so I am making the assumption that people search on their desktops – at work – and then in their lunch breaks or commutes they are making the purchases on their phones.
